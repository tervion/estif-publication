## 🔬 Scientific Approach

### Physical Intuition

Imagine 3D space as a flat sheet moving through 4D space. In empty space the sheet
is perpendicular to the 4th dimension. Near a massive object, the sheet **tilts** —
exactly as a rubber membrane deforms under a heavy ball. A 3D observer on the sheet
can only measure the component of 4D friction that **projects back onto their surface**.
The rest is hidden in the invisible direction. This projection is the suppression.

### The Lensing Equation

```
θ_ESTIF = θ_GR × (1 + β(r) × R_s/(2r))
```

### The Key Advance: β Is Now Derived, Not Fitted

Previous versions used β = 0.05 (fitted to data). This version derives β from the
geometry of the 4D tilt:

```
Tilt model:   sin(θ) = (R_s/r)^n
Suppression:  β(r)   = cos(θ) = √(1 - (R_s/r)^(2n))
```

Where `n` is the **tilt exponent** — the only free parameter, now constrained
by observation rather than fitted.

### How n Is Constrained

The tilt exponent n controls how steeply mass tilts 3D space into the 4th dimension:

- **Small n** → gentle tilt → small suppression → larger 4D effect visible in 3D
- **Large n** → steep tilt → strong suppression → most of 4D effect hidden

EHT M87\* observations constrain: **n = 0.05 to 0.215** (within 1σ)

With geometric suppression, the shadow prediction is now **consistent** with EHT:

```
EHT observed:    42.0 ± 3.0 μas
GR prediction:   39.7 μas   (0.77σ from EHT — consistent)
ESTIF (n=0.05):  42.3 μas   (0.11σ from EHT — consistent ✅)
ESTIF (n=0.215): 45.0 μas   (0.94σ from EHT — consistent ✅)
```

Previous v2.0 result (β=0.05 fitted): 1.67% deviation, marginal.
Previous v2.0 result (β=1.0): 33% deviation, ruled out.
**Current v3.0 result: Within 1σ across entire constrained n range.**

With the ISCO correction (GWs generated at r = 3R_s, not at the horizon):

```
LIGO precision: 1 ms  (signal below threshold)
LISA precision: 10 μs (signal well above threshold)
```

**The entire EHT-consistent n range predicts LISA-detectable GW delays.**

## 🎓 Scientific Context

### Motivation

General Relativity has been extensively validated in weak fields (solar system,
binary pulsars) but remains less constrained near black hole horizons. The Event
Horizon Telescope's imaging of M87\* and LIGO's detection of gravitational waves
open new windows for testing gravity in extreme environments.

### Relation to Standard Physics

ESTIF-Gravity:
- ✅ **Preserves:** All weak-field GR predictions
- ✅ **Uses:** Standard ΛCDM cosmology
- ✅ **Derives:** β from 4D hypersurface tilt geometry
- 🔬 **Tests:** Whether friction-like corrections appear in strong fields
- ❌ **Not claiming:** To solve dark matter, dark energy, or replace quantum gravity

### Previous Work

An earlier version (ESTIF-FD) attempted to derive cosmic expansion from 4D flow
dynamics. This was **ruled out by supernova data:**

- ESTIF-FD: χ² = 1428
- ΛCDM: χ² = 376
- Ratio: 3.8× worse

**Current approach** accepts standard cosmology and tests only strong-field modifications.
